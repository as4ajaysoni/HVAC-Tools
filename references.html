Saturated Vapor Pressure

Antoine Equation

Used to calculate saturated vapor pressure at a given temperature.

log10(Psat) = A - B/(C + T)

Where:

  • Psat = Saturated vapor pressure (mmHg)
  • T = Temperature (°C)
  • A, B, C = Antoine coefficients for water
Psat(kPa) = Psat(mmHg) × 0.133322
Note: Valid for temperature range 1°C to 100°C. For temperatures outside this range, more complex formulations like the Goff-Gratch equation would be required.

Atmospheric Pressure

Barometric Pressure with Altitude

Calculates atmospheric pressure at a given altitude using the hypsometric equation.

P = P0 × (1 - L×h/T0)g×M/(R×L)

Simplified for standard atmosphere:

P = 101.325 × (1 - 0.0065×h/288.15)5.255

Where:

  • P = Atmospheric pressure at altitude h (kPa)
  • P0 = Sea level standard pressure (101.325 kPa)
  • h = Altitude (m)
  • L = Temperature lapse rate (0.0065 K/m)
  • T0 = Standard temperature at sea level (288.15 K)

Humidity Ratio

From Dry Bulb and Wet Bulb Temperatures

W = [(1093 - 0.556×Twb)×Ws,wb - 0.240×(Tdb - Twb)] / [1093 + 0.444×Tdb - Twb]

Where:

  • W = Humidity ratio (kgwater/kgdry_air)
  • Tdb = Dry bulb temperature (°C)
  • Twb = Wet bulb temperature (°C)
  • Ws,wb = Saturated humidity ratio at wet bulb temperature

From Dry Bulb Temperature and Relative Humidity

W = 0.621945 × (φ×Ps,db) / (P - φ×Ps,db)

Where:

  • φ = Relative humidity (decimal fraction)
  • Ps,db = Saturated vapor pressure at dry bulb temperature
  • P = Atmospheric pressure

From Dew Point Temperature

W = 0.621945 × Ps,dp / (P - Ps,dp)

Where Ps,dp = Saturated vapor pressure at dew point temperature

Relative Humidity

From Dry Bulb Temperature and Humidity Ratio

φ = (Pw / Ps,db) × 100%

Where:

  • Pw = Partial pressure of water vapor = W×P/(0.621945 + W)
  • Ps,db = Saturated vapor pressure at dry bulb temperature

From Dry Bulb and Dew Point Temperatures

φ = (Ps,dp / Ps,db) × 100%

Where Ps,dp = Saturated vapor pressure at dew point temperature

Thermodynamic Properties

Specific Enthalpy

h = cp,air×Tdb + W×(hfg + cp,vapor×Tdb)

Simplified form:

h = 1.006×Tdb + W×(2501 + 1.86×Tdb)

Where:

  • h = Specific enthalpy (kJ/kgdry_air)
  • cp,vapor = Specific heat of water vapor ≈ 1.86 kJ/(kg·K)

Specific Volume

v = Rair×TK / Pdry

Where:

  • v = Specific volume (m³/kgdry_air)
  • TK = Absolute temperature (K) = Tdb + 273.15
  • Pdry = Partial pressure of dry air = P - Pw

Temperature Conversions

Dew Point Temperature

Calculated by inverting the Antoine equation:

Tdp = B/(A - log10(Pw/0.133322)) - C

Where Pw = Partial pressure of water vapor (kPa)

References & Standards

Primary References:

  • ASHRAE Handbook - Fundamentals: American Society of Heating, Refrigerating and Air-Conditioning Engineers
  • ISO 7730: Ergonomics of the thermal environment
  • Cengel, Y.A. & Ghajar, A.J.: Heat and Mass Transfer: Fundamentals and Applications
  • Mills, A.F.: Basic Heat and Mass Transfer

Standard Values:

  • Standard Atmospheric Pressure: 101.325 kPa at sea level
  • Standard Temperature: 15°C (288.15 K) at sea level
  • Temperature Lapse Rate: 6.5 K/km in troposphere

Applicability:

  • Valid for temperatures between -50°C and 50°C
  • Accurate for altitudes up to 10,000 meters
  • Assumes ideal gas behavior for air and water vapor mixtures
  • Antoine equation valid for water temperatures 1°C to 100°C

Note: These formulas are based on standard psychrometric relationships. For applications requiring high precision, consider using more complex formulations or reference tables from ASHRAE.
